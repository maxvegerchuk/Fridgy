# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Families and households: typically one primary person managing groceries and cooking for 2+ people. They open the app in three moments: at the grocery store checking what they already have, before cooking to see what they can make with current pantry stock, and at home updating items after shopping. Secondary users are household members (partners, roommates) who share lists and pantry access. Not single-person productivity power users; the dominant context is domestic, practical, often one-handed and on the move.

## Product Purpose

Fridgy is a household kitchen management app. It reduces friction around three core problems: not knowing what's in the pantry (so food gets wasted or bought twice), forgetting what's needed at the store (so shopping is inefficient), and not knowing what to cook with what you have (so people default to ordering out). Success looks like: less food waste, fewer "what do we have?" moments, and fewer redundant grocery runs.

## Brand Personality

Fresh, clear, trusted. Warm without being precious. Confident without being cold. The voice is domestic intelligence, not tech product.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS**: purple/blue gradients, hero metrics, dashboard clichés, feature-announcement-style UI. Fridgy is a household tool, not a B2B product.
- **Meal kit brands**: HelloFresh / Gousto energy, loud promotional photography, heavy CTAs, urgency patterns. Fridgy is a utility, not a commerce funnel.
- **Fitness/nutrition apps**: calorie counters, macro bars, clinical white-and-green, judgment-heavy metrics. Food here is pleasure and practicality, not optimization.

## Design Principles

1. **Surface the food, not the feature.** Real content (ingredient names, recipe images, pantry status) carries the UI. Chrome stays minimal. A screen that shows off its navigation structure is failing.

2. **Glanceable at the grocery store.** Information hierarchy must work in 2 seconds, one-handed, in bright light. Dense secondary details hide behind a tap; primary status (have it / need it / can make it) reads instantly.

3. **Shared by default.** Every feature is designed as if two people might be looking at the same data. Shared shopping lists and pantry access are first-class, not afterthoughts.

4. **Consumer confidence, not power-user efficiency.** Reference point is Airbnb: image-led, easy to scan, no learning curve. Not Linear. Broad household appeal over niche productivity density.

5. **Restraint builds trust.** A quiet interface that gets out of the way signals that the product is reliable. No gamification, no streaks, no nudges. The reward is a well-stocked kitchen.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Sufficient color contrast for all text and interactive elements. Never rely on color alone to convey state (use icons or labels alongside color indicators). Support system font scaling for text elements. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for any animations added.
